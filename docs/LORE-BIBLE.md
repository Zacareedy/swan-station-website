# DHARMA Lore Bible

Canon facts from the LOST universe used in this project. All new content must be consistent with this document. When inventing new lore (station logs, personnel notes, etc.), stay within the boundaries established here.

-----

## The DHARMA Initiative

- **Full name:** Department of Heuristics And Research on Material Applications
- **Founded:** 1970
- **Founders:** Gerald and Karen DeGroot (University of Michigan)
- **Funder:** The Hanso Foundation (Alvar Hanso)
- **Purpose:** Large-scale scientific research across multiple disciplines — stated as advancing human knowledge; actual purpose involves the Valenzetti Equation
- **Operating period:** ~1970–1992 (purge by the Others), though some stations continued unknowingly
- **Logo:** Octagonal dharma wheel with station-specific symbol in centre

## The Swan Station (Station 3)

- **Location:** Island, sub-jungle, sub-level construction
- **Purpose:** Electromagnetic energy containment via periodic operator input
- **Established:** 1980 (following The Incident of April 23, 1980)
- **Intranet node:** SWN-7, Sublevel B
- **Protocol:** Protocol 23 — input sequence every 108 minutes
- **Software version:** v6.1.1

### The Incident (April 23, 1980)

An uncontrolled electromagnetic event during scheduled maintenance in Sub-level C. Casualties and cause are classified (ORDER V.K.). The result was the establishment of Protocol 23 and the Swan’s conversion to indefinite occupied operation.

### The Failsafe

Located in Sub-level C, housing F-7. A key that, when turned, triggers an uncontrolled discharge — the option of absolute last resort. (In the show, this was used by Desmond in the season 2 finale, causing the sky to turn purple.)

### The Blast Door Map

A hand-drawn map compiled over years by operators Radzinsky and Kelvin on the blast doors of the station. Contains annotations, speculative notes, and ultraviolet-visible ink. One annotation: “I AM HERE.” Origin unknown.

## The Numbers: 4 8 15 16 23 42

- **In the show:** A numerical sequence with seemingly supernatural properties. Associated with Hurley’s lottery win, Rousseau’s distress signal, the hatch, and ultimately the Valenzetti Equation.
- **In this site:** The correct input sequence for Protocol 23. Never displayed as plaintext in the UI — discovered only through lore and ARG.
- **Sum:** 108 (the interval in minutes)
- **Origin:** The Valenzetti Equation — each number is a core factor

-----

## The Valenzetti Equation

- **Author:** Enzo Valenzetti, 1962
- **Commissioned by:** The UN after the Cuban Missile Crisis
- **Purpose:** Predicts the exact date of human extinction based on core environmental and human factors
- **The six factors:** Represented by the six numbers
- **DHARMA’s mission:** Change the values of one or more factors to delay or prevent the predicted outcome
- **The Swan’s role:** Preventing a discharge event that would accelerate Factor 4 (not specified)
- **Key insight used in this site:** Entering the numbers does not change the equation — it only maintains the status quo. The clock is being kept running, not stopped.

## Key Personnel

### Marvin Candle (also Pierre Chang)

Technical Officer at multiple stations. Hosted orientation films under various pseudonyms. Lost his arm (in some accounts) during The Incident. Real name: Pierre Chang. The site uses “Candle, M.” as the tech officer.

### Gerald and Karen DeGroot

Founders of the DHARMA Initiative. University of Michigan. Never appear directly in the show — known from orientation films and records. Chief Wickmund in this site is an invented character in the same administrative tier.

### Kelvin Joe Inman (V.K. / “I.P.” ambiguity in site)

Former CIA/military operative who became a Swan Station operator. Taught Desmond to maintain the station. His departure (death) left Desmond alone in the station. In this site he is referenced as “V.K.” (Kelvin, reversed initials) and appears in file notes, the morse decode message, and the VK-108 file signature.

### Stuart Radzinsky

Previous operator who (in the show) co-created the blast door map with Kelvin, then killed himself. Referenced in the `RADZINSKY` terminal command.

### Desmond David Hume

Not directly named in the site (he is the “current” implied operator the player is standing in for). His story arc — alone in the hatch, pushing the button every 108 minutes, gradually losing faith — is the emotional core of what the site is evoking.

### “Penny” (Penelope Widmore)

Referenced in the `PENNY` terminal command with the phrase “Not Penny’s Boat” — Charlie Pace’s final message before drowning in The Looking Glass.

## Lore Conventions Used in This Site

### Invented but canon-consistent elements

- **Cycle numbering:** The site uses “Cycle 10894” as the current cycle. At one cycle per 108 minutes, this represents roughly 20+ years of operation, consistent with the station’s 1980 establishment and the show’s 2004 setting.
- **Chief Wickmund:** An invented DHARMA administrator. Name chosen in the same register as other DHARMA personnel.
- **NODE SWN-7:** The intranet node designation is invented. Consistent with the show’s obsession with specific serial identifiers.
- **ORDER V.K.:** A redaction order attributed to Kelvin — explains why files are classified even internally.
- **Sonar degradation:** Consistent with the show’s hints about the island’s anomalous properties affecting equipment.
- **Relief team ETA 540 hours:** ~22.5 days. A believable rotation period that also contains the digits 5, 4, 0 — subtle numerological nod.

### Things deliberately omitted

- The actual numbers are never shown in the UI (by design — this is the core ARG mechanic)
- The Others, Jacob, the Smoke Monster’s true nature — referenced cryptically but not explained (appropriate for the intranet’s limited knowledge)
- Season 3+ lore (the purge, Widmore, etc.) — the station is presented as operational circa 2004, before the castaways arrive

## Voice and Tone

All in-site text should feel like it was written by DHARMA bureaucrats in the late 1970s–early 1990s:

- **Formal, clipped, institutional.** No personality. No contractions in official documents.
- **Implied menace without explanation.** “Do not go outside” with no further detail is more effective than explanation.
- **Redaction as a feature.** `[CLASSIFIED]`, `[REDACTED — ORDER V.K.]`, `██████` — these convey that there is more, without lying.
- **The numbers are sacred.** Never trivialised, never explained away.
- **Deadpan urgency.** Everything is stated as fact. No exclamation marks in official documents (only in alarm states).

### Example of good tone:

> “The outside air was last independently tested in 1987. Protocol mandates internal operations only. We have learned there are additional reasons not to go outside. They are not documented here.”

### Example of bad tone (do not write like this):

> “WARNING! The outside is DANGEROUS! Don’t go out there! Something terrible might happen!”
